What to try.

  1. Click Burst (10). Watch dots flow down both pipelines.
  2. Mid-flow, click 💥 Crash. Both pipelines freeze.
    • Left: every dot still in transit turns red — lost forever. The bus has no record of them.
    • Right: dots that already reached the eventOutbox turn yellow — queued. They survived because the row is in SQLite. Only dots that hadn't yet reached the transaction commit are lost.
  3. Click ↻ Restart.
    • Left: nothing happens. The lost events are gone.
    • Right: the polling worker resumes and drains the outbox — yellow dots turn green and reach all four lanes (background, projection, observability, realtime).

The key difference isn't extra plumbing — it's where the durability boundary sits. In the current design, an event becomes durable only after it has successfully reached its sink. In Wonderlands, an event becomes durable at the same instant as the business change that produced it, because they share a SQL transaction. Delivery is then just a worker draining a queue.
